eLearning Module

How to Be Successful in College

A self-paced module helping first-year and conditionally admitted students learn how to email instructors and staff respectfully and effectively.

The Problem

Many first-year college students arrive on campus with limited experience writing professional emails. This often results in unclear, overly casual, or incomplete messages to professors and campus staff, which can lead to missed information, delayed responses, and weakened academic support.

These students, coming from a range of educational and cultural backgrounds, may be unfamiliar with the tone, structure, or expectations of effective academic communication. Common gaps include not knowing how to address a professor, what to include in the body of a message, or how to ask for help in a respectful, confident way.

Instructional Design Approach

The module is grounded in adult learning principles, with short, focused lessons and real-life scenarios. It offers students simple, repeatable strategies for real academic situations, including an accordion overview of best practices, flashcards and knowledge checks to reinforce feedback and mistakes, a downloadable job aid for continued use, and a final reflection prompt to encourage application.

Deliverables

Students walk away with a quick-reference job aid summarizing the six most important habits for emailing professors successfully.

Results

Students who complete this module report feeling more confident reaching out to instructors and staff, and are better able to write emails that get a clear, timely response instead of getting lost in an inbox. The module has become a simple reference point students return to throughout their time in college, not just in their first year.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the key components of a respectful, effective email
  • Recognize common mistakes in tone, structure, and clarity
  • Practice editing or improving email examples
  • Build confidence in communicating with professors and campus staff
