Onboarding Course

Empower Program

A self-paced module onboarding students into Lipscomb University's Empower Program, an academic support initiative for conditionally admitted students.

The Problem

Conditionally admitted students often face transitional challenges as they adjust to college-level academics and expectations. Many enter with limited familiarity with academic probation policies, available resources, or how to advocate for themselves proactively.

Additionally, many begin the program without a clear understanding of what the Empower Program is or why they've been placed in it, leading to confusion, disengagement, or lack of buy-in. Without clarity and coaching, students may struggle to stay in good standing, access support, or meet the terms of their admission.

Instructional Design Approach

This module uses a supportive, student-centered instructional design approach to clarify policy without overwhelming students. Institutional policy language from the Empower Agreement was translated into friendly, actionable steps.

Scenarios and knowledge checks were included to reinforce critical content, and actual photos of Academic Success Center staff and spaces help students visually connect with the department supporting their success. The module closes with a digital commitment to reinforce personal responsibility and program accountability.

Deliverables

The slide deck for this module is being finalized and will be linked here soon.

Results

By breaking the Empower Agreement into clear, digestible pieces, this module gives students both understanding and a sense of control over their academic standing. Students come away with a clearer picture of what's expected of them and feel more equipped to advocate for themselves and use the resources available on campus.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain the purpose of the Empower Program and the conditions of their admission
  • Identify required actions and expectations related to academic standing, course load, and coaching sessions
  • Recognize key academic support resources available at Lipscomb
  • Understand the consequences of falling below program expectations
  • Commit to participation through a digital agreement and reflect on strategies for success
